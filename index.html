Intro

Hi! I'm Alessandro, currently a robotics Ph.D. student at the University of Pisa. I work on autonomous manipulation and optimal trajectory planning for collaborative robots.

I received a master in Robotics and Automation (2018) and a bachelor in Electronics (2015) at the University of Pisa, both cum laude.

If you want to know more about my research, projects, and publications, please follow the associated links.

Research

My research mainly focuses on optimal trajectory planning and manipulation planning for collaborative robots with applications for Industry 4.0.
I'm working on a hierarchical multi-level manipulation planning approach, in which a symbolic search at a more abstract level of representation is used to guide the generation of possibly complex manipulation policies, based on combinations of elementary actions. During my Ph.D, I have also worked on data-driven grasp planning algorithms, able of transferring the human grasping skills to robots to enable grasping of generic unknown objects, and on multi-vehicle coordination algorithms.
My work resulted in publications on leading robotics journals, such as RA-L and RA-M, and on international conferences such as ICRA, IROS, CASE, and Humanoids.

Time-optimal path tracking
for jerk-controlled robots

Abstract

This letter presents a new approach to solve the time-optimal path tracking under limited joint range and bounds on velocity, acceleration, and jerk. To obtain smooth and continuous accelerations, with beneficial effects for the load and wear on the actuators but a limited impact on performance, we state the minimum-time path tracking problem with the jerk as the control input. The main contribution of this letter is a formulation that includes the jerk constraints in the optimization problem and that, even if the resulting non-linear programming problem is non-convex, allows to perform an efficient and reliable convex relaxation using McCormick envelopes. Simulations and experimental tests on two 7-DoF manipulators have been carried out to show the benefits of the proposed approach and to compare it to state-of-the-art techniques.
